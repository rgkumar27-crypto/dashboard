From spreadsheet to interactive impact platform.

This proposal reimagines Compudopt reporting as a donor-friendly dashboard: a place where users can understand where devices move, where schools are impacted, and why the work matters without reading rows of spreadsheet data.

Open the dashboard View Compudopt

What this solves

  • Makes program data more accessible and digestible for donors, judges, and partners.
  • Transforms static spreadsheets into map-based storytelling with clear metrics.
  • Combines impact locations and donation locations in one interface.
  • Shows significance by moving from state view to city view to school view.
Core message: Compudopt should not just report data. It should let people see impact.

Framework

How the spreadsheet becomes a dashboard

Before

Rows of operational data are useful internally, but hard for outside audiences to interpret quickly.

  • Donation sites and impact sites are disconnected.
  • School-level outcomes are buried in tables.
  • Donors cannot easily visualize scale.

After

The same information becomes an interactive donor-facing platform.

  • National totals anchor the story.
  • State and city drilldowns make the data legible.
  • Donation locations connect giving to visible impact.

Data model

A future real version could map spreadsheet columns directly into these dashboard objects.

  • `state`, `city`, `school_name`
  • `students_reached`, `computers_distributed`
  • `donation_site_name`, `donation_site_type`
  • `impact_note`, `photo_url`
